Classic Spaghetti with Tomato Sauce

Ingredients:

  • For the sauce:
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
    • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
    • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
    • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to reduce acidity)
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1 teaspoon dried basil
    • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for a bit of heat)
    • Fresh basil leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • For the spaghetti:
    • 1 pound spaghetti
    • Salt for boiling water
    • Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the sauce:
    1. In a large skillet or saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
    2. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes.
    3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes, stirring frequently to avoid burning.
    4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, and tomato paste.
    5. Add the sugar (if using), oregano, basil, thyme, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using).
    6. Bring the sauce to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer gently for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. This allows the flavors to meld together.
  2. Cook the spaghetti:
    1. While the sauce is simmering, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    2. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
    3. Drain the spaghetti, reserving about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water.
  3. Combine and serve:
    1. If the sauce is too thick, add a bit of the reserved pasta cooking water to reach your desired consistency.
    2. Toss the cooked spaghetti with the sauce until well combined.
    3. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese.

Enjoy your homemade spaghetti with tomato sauce!
